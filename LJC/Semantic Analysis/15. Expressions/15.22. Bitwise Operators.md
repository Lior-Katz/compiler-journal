# [15.22.](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/jls/se26/html/jls-15.html#jls-15.22) Bitwise Operators 

##### [15.22.1.](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/jls/se26/html/jls-15.html#jls-15.22.1) Integer Bitwise Operators `&`, `^`, and `|`
When both operands of an operator `&`, `^`, or `|` are of a type that is convertible ([§5.1.8](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/jls/se26/html/jls-5.html#jls-5.1.8 "5.1.8. Unboxing Conversion")) to a primitive integral type, binary numeric promotion is first performed on the operands ([§5.6](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/jls/se26/html/jls-5.html#jls-5.6 "5.6. Numeric Contexts")).

- [ ] The type of the bitwise operator expression is the promoted type of the operands.
##### [15.22.2.](https://docs.oracle.com/javase/specs/jls/se26/html/jls-15.html#jls-15.22.2) Boolean Logical Operators `&`, `^`, and `|`
- [x] When both operands of a `&`, `^`, or `|` operator are of type `boolean` or `Boolean`, then the type of the bitwise operator expression is `boolean`.
